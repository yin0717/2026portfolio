About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Tri-Institutional Chemical Biology Program at Weill Cornell Graduate School / Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, where I work in the laboratory of Dr. Xuejun Jiang.

My research focuses on cancer metabolism, ferroptosis regulation, and the survival pathways that allow tumor cells to resist oxidative stress and small-molecule inducers. I combine mammalian cell line engineering, CRISPR-based perturbations, biochemical assays, and in vivo models to uncover actionable mechanisms in oncology.

This summer (2026) I am a Ph.D. intern in the Translational Medicine Oncology group at Genentech, Inc., working on biomarker validation and drug efficacy confirmation.

Research Focus

◆

Cancer Metabolism & Ferroptosis

Investigating how oncogenic PI3K-Akt-mTOR signaling and lipid remodeling pathways (PLA2G2F, GPX4) govern ferroptotic cell death resistance in endometrial and bladder cancers.

◆

CRISPR & Cell Engineering

Designing and executing CRISPR-based perturbations, lentiviral transductions, and stable cell line engineering to dissect gene function and pathway crosstalk in cancer models.

◆

Drug MoA & Biomarker Discovery

Developing robust biochemical and cellular assays to evaluate on/off-target drug effects, prioritize screening hits, and validate biomarkers for clinical translation in oncology.
